BUA 235- Nick Lunn

Sigma Phi Epsilon

Founded in 1901 in Virginia

Maine Alpha est. 1948

Member since April 2014



At first I wanted to have nothing to do with Fraternity life because of the typical fraternity stereotypes of parties and hazing, but the guys down at SigEp were persistent and kept after me to consider joining. After hanging out with some of them and getting to know what SigEp stood for and what they were about, it really opened my mind up to fraternity life and SigEp especially. SigEp has a dry house, so no parties, and we pride ourselves in our high standards of academic success by requiring members to achieve and maintain at least a 3.0 GPA, with our chapter GPA averaging around a 3.4.

We also pride ourselves in a principle we call Sound Body which means that we strive to take care of our body and live healthy lifestyles. In intramurals, we have won the overall intramural award, the BC Kent, 8 of the last 9 years, with winning the last 4. We also strive to accumulate lots of community service hours and help out our local community. Many of our members hold leadership positions on campus such as in Student Government, Senior Skulls, Sophomore Owls, and many more. Our chapter and SigEp nationally have adopted the "Balanced Man" principle that stands that our members strive to be balanced in all areas of life, specifically in 4 areas: Athlete, Scholar, Gentleman, and Leader.

This principle also mandates that all chapters have a rolling recruitment process meaning that we may recruit potential new members at any point during the academic year, and not just during a "Rush Week." Potential new members received a "bid" which has been approved democratically by the Chapter as a whole, and have up to one calendar year to accept membership, at which point they are a full member of SigEp and have all the rights and privileges as any other member.
