BUA 235- Joe Clark

Stay in School Kids

Chapter 1: Computer Engineering

My first major was Computer Engineering because I thought I was tech savvy, but I quickly realized that I was not truly computer savvy because most of these kids that I was in class with had built their own computers in middle school while I was playing sports and video games during that time. I did enjoy the programming aspect of it the most as this assignment of making a webpage using HTML reminds me of the summer going into college and that first semester.

Chapter 2: Kinesiology & Physical Education

I quickly switched into KPE after my first semester because I've always enjoyed health and fitness and learning how the human body works. I chose the Exercise Science option because I wanted to pursue my DPT (Doctor of Physical Therapy) after UMaine. I truly enjoyed my time in the KPE program as all the classes were (mostly) fun and enjoyable and even some classes were essentially a glorified gym class (KPE 253,426). However, as I got to the close of my junior year and planning on applying to grad schools, I quickly realized how expensive it would be to go for 3 years to schools like Husson or UNE without any aid and only receive a $60,000 salary. So here comes major change #3. Well, adding a major.

Chapter 3: Accounting

In Spring of 2016 I declared a second degree, Accounting, because I am handy with numbers and excel and I figured this major would fit my semi-introvertish nature. I only had 12 credits left to towards my KPE degree so I figured I'd just finish that up slowly over the next couple years as I worked towards my Accounting degree. My hopes are to get my CPA license and then work my way up to a CFO by my early- to mid-30s.
